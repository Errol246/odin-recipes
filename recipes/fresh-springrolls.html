Fresh springrolls

Home

Description

Fresh springrolls are a Vietnamese delicacy. As opposed to their fried counterparts, and as the name implies, these springrolls are uncooked and as such are enjoyed cold. They typically contain a type of meat, such as pork or shrimp, as well as leafy lettuce and fresh herbs, and they are dipped in a delicious hoisin peanut sauce.

Ingredients

  • Rice paper
  • Shrimp
  • Lettuce
  • Shiitake mushrooms
  • Rice noodles
  • Coriander
  • Mint
  • Thai basil
  • Hoisin sauce
  • Smooth peanut butter
  • Sugar
  • Soy sauce

Steps

  1. Soak the rice noodles for 30 minutes, and prepare the remaining ingredients meanwhile
  2. In a separate bowl, soak your shiitake mushrooms – also for 30 minutes
  3. Boil a kettle full of water and set aside
  4. De-vein your shrimp and fry them till they're perfectly cooked
  5. Wash all your greens
  6. Tear the lettuce into reasonably sized leaves
  7. Remove the leaves from the stems of your herbs
  8. When done soaking, de-stem the mushrooms and cut them into strips
  9. Pour the water from the kettle onto a flat plate
  10. Gently soak the rice paper into the now lukewarm water on both sides, then put on a different plate
  11. Add your fillings, starting with a piece of lettuce, some rice noodles, mushrooms and an even mix of the three different herbs
  12. Fold both sides of the rice paper a third of the way, then half close the springroll, making sure to leave some space to add the final ingredient
  13. Add three or four shrimps to the remaining open space – make sure to place them with their backs facing outwards
  14. Repeat this process until your have your desired amount of fresh springrolls
  15. Put some hoisin sauce and peanut butter into a small pot and bring it to a boil, then lower the heat to a simmer
  16. Add some soy sauce and sugar to the mixture and dissolve
  17. Pour the sauce into a container for dipping, dip your fresh springroll into the sauce and enjoy!
